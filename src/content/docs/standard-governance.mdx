            <p>
               This page sets out the governance model, governance principles, scope boundaries, and structural relationships that govern maintenance of the ARAF standard.
            </p>

            <section id="governance-model">
               <h2>Governance Model</h2>
               <p>
                  The standard operates under a Founding Stewardship governance model. Governance authority is held by Institute for Autonomous Governance Pty Ltd, with methodological stewardship by the originating author. This structure ensures methodological coherence during the period in which the standard is establishing its institutional foundation: the first assessments, the first accredited assessors, and the first institutional adoptions.
               </p>
               <p>
                  The open licence (CC BY 4.0) ensures that the methodology is publicly available and independently reviewable regardless of governance structure. Regulatory bodies, assessors, and institutional users can reference and build upon the standard without proprietary barriers.
               </p>
               <p>
                  The governance model follows the trajectory established by every durable institutional standard: concentrated expertise at inception, broadening to institutional representation as the ecosystem matures. Governance transition is triggered by observable ecosystem conditions, not calendar time.
               </p>
            </section>

            <section id="governance-stages">
               <h2>Governance Stages</h2>
               <div className="governance-stage-grid">
                  <section className="governance-stage" aria-labelledby="stage-founding-stewardship">
                     <h3 id="stage-founding-stewardship">Founding Stewardship (current)</h3>
                     <p>
                        Governance authority held by the Institute. Methodological integrity maintained by the originating author. Structural separation between standard governance and assessment operations operative from first publication. All five governance commitments (purpose primacy, version discipline, public transparency, methodological consistency, institutional usability) are permanent and apply at every governance stage.
                     </p>
                  </section>

                  <section className="governance-stage" aria-labelledby="stage-institutional-advisory">
                     <h3 id="stage-institutional-advisory">Institutional Advisory</h3>
                     <p>
                        Triggered by defined ecosystem conditions. An advisory body incorporating technical, institutional, and regulatory perspectives contributes to methodology development and amendment processes. The advisory body provides input on material revisions. Governance authority remains with the Institute.
                     </p>
                  </section>

                  <section className="governance-stage" aria-labelledby="stage-independent-governance">
                     <h3 id="stage-independent-governance">Independent Governance</h3>
                     <p>
                        Triggered by defined ecosystem maturity criteria. Governance transitions to an independent board with defined composition, meeting cadence, public reporting, and multi-party amendment consultation. The originating author retains a defined role. Governance operates by constitution and board resolution.
                     </p>
                  </section>
               </div>
               <p>
                  Transition criteria between stages are specified in the Institute's governing documents. Each transition is documented and published.
               </p>
            </section>

            <section id="five-governance-principles">
               <h2>Five Governance Principles</h2>
               <ol>
                  <li>
                     <strong>Purpose primacy.</strong> The ARAF standard exists to make governance of autonomous systems measurable, comparable, and institutionally usable. Every governance decision affecting the published standard is evaluated against this purpose. The standard is not maintained to serve the commercial interests of any single participant in the ecosystem.
                  </li>
                  <li>
                     <strong>Version discipline.</strong> Each public version of the standard is clearly identified, dated, and distinguishable from prior and future versions. Institutional audiences citing the standard in procurement requirements, regulatory guidance, or insurance underwriting guidelines must be able to reference a specific version with confidence that its content is stable.
                  </li>
                  <li>
                     <strong>Public transparency.</strong> The current version, governance status, amendment history, and citation format are visible to all users of the framework. The standard is not maintained behind access restrictions.
                  </li>
                  <li>
                     <strong>Methodological consistency.</strong> Changes to the framework preserve the internal coherence of the methodology. Dimensional definitions, scoring logic, multiplier thresholds, and certification tier boundaries are not altered informally or ambiguously. Material changes follow a defined amendment process and are documented in the version history.
                  </li>
                  <li>
                     <strong>Institutional usability.</strong> The standard remains usable by the institutional audiences for whom it is designed: boards, regulators, insurers, investors, enterprise procurement teams, and accredited assessors. Changes that would impair comparability across assessment periods or break backward compatibility with prior certification decisions require explicit justification and transition guidance.
                  </li>
               </ol>
            </section>

            <section id="scope-of-governance">
               <h2>Scope of Governance</h2>
               <h3>Standard governance covers:</h3>
               <ul>
                  <li>Version publication and maintenance</li>
                  <li>Methodology updates and clarifications</li>
                  <li>Interpretive guidance for assessors</li>
                  <li>Governance notes for future revisions</li>
                  <li>Publication and maintenance of version history</li>
                  <li>Stewardship of public documentation (standard specification, dimensional guidance, GBI methodology, certification tier definitions)</li>
               </ul>
               <h3>Standard governance does not constitute:</h3>
               <ul>
                  <li>Assessor accreditation (governed by the Assessor Accreditation Standard)</li>
                  <li>Certification issuance (conducted by accredited assessors, not the standard maintainer)</li>
                  <li>Evidence infrastructure certification (governed by a supplementary specification; publication pending)</li>
                  <li>Any statutory or regulatory authority</li>
               </ul>
            </section>

            <section id="relationship-to-certification">
               <h2>Relationship to Certification</h2>
               <p>
                  The governance of the standard and the operation of certification functions are related but distinct.
               </p>
               <p>
                  The standard defines the methodology: six dimensions, multiplier logic, GBI scoring, evidence requirements, and certification tier thresholds. Certification depends on assessment practice, accreditation discipline, and independence requirements that operate on top of the published standard.
               </p>
               <p>
                  This separation follows established governance precedent. ISO maintains standards; accredited certification bodies conduct assessments. PCI Security Standards Council maintains PCI DSS; Qualified Security Assessors conduct compliance assessments. ARAF follows the same structural separation.
               </p>
               <p>
                  The standard maintainer in its advisory services capacity (Venture Bench Pty Ltd) operates under the same independence requirements as all accredited assessors. Advisory and assessment functions are structurally separated: separate personnel, separate engagement terms, and documented independence protocols. No assessment conducted by the standard maintainer carries greater or lesser authority than an assessment conducted by any other accredited assessor.
               </p>
            </section>

            <section id="relationship-to-the-assessor-ecosystem">
               <h2>Relationship to the Assessor Ecosystem</h2>
               <p>
                  The ARAF certification ecosystem depends on independent assessors with demonstrated competence in governance assessment, ARAF methodology proficiency, and structural independence from the organisations they assess.
               </p>
               <p>Assessor accreditation requires:</p>
               <ul>
                  <li><strong>Governance assessment competence:</strong> demonstrated understanding of corporate governance, risk management, legal accountability frameworks, and AI governance principles</li>
                  <li><strong>ARAF methodology proficiency:</strong> demonstrated ability to apply the six-dimension framework, interpret dimensional evidence, apply multiplier logic, calculate GBI scores, and produce assessment reports meeting the standard</li>
                  <li><strong>Independence:</strong> no material financial or organisational relationship with assessed organisations that would compromise assessment objectivity</li>
                  <li><strong>Professional accountability:</strong> submission to quality assurance review, acceptance of assessment dispute procedures, and agreement to accreditation withdrawal for sustained quality failure</li>
               </ul>
               <p>
                  Advisory organisations may hold assessor accreditation provided that advisory and assessment functions are structurally separated: different personnel, separate engagement terms, and documented independence protocols.
               </p>
            </section>

            <section id="relationship-to-evidence-infrastructure-certification">
               <h2>Relationship to Evidence Infrastructure Certification</h2>
               <p>
                  Governance infrastructure platforms (data governance platforms, AI gateways, action admission systems) may seek ARAF Evidence Infrastructure certification, demonstrating that their audit outputs satisfy ARAF evidentiary standards.
               </p>
               <p>
                  This is a product-level designation, not an organisational assessment. It certifies that the platform's governance telemetry meets the authenticity, integrity, traceability, and exportability requirements of the ARAF evidence standard.
               </p>
               <p>
                  Organisations deploying certified infrastructure inherit an evidentiary advantage in their own ARAF assessments: the evidence their infrastructure produces has already been verified against the standard, qualifying as Tier 1 (infrastructure-generated) evidence for assessment purposes.
               </p>
               <p>
                  Evidence Infrastructure certification is maintained as a supplementary specification to the core ARAF standard. Publication of the supplementary specification is pending.
               </p>
            </section>
