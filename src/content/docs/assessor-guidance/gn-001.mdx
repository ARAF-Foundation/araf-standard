*Assessor Guidance Note GN-001 · Agentic Risk Architecture Framework v3.0*

---

This guidance note defines the end-to-end operational sequence for conducting an ARAF governance assessment. It applies from engagement setup through certification determination, report delivery, and post-assessment obligations.

GN-001 is the foundational assessor methodology document. It must be read in conjunction with the [Assessor Accreditation Standard](/assessor-guidance/assessor-accreditation-standard/), [GN-002: Cross-Border Deployment Assessment](/assessor-guidance/gn-002/), [GN-003: Governance Coherence Assessment](/assessor-guidance/gn-003/), and the normative specification documents referenced in the cross-reference section below.

![ARAF Assessment Lifecycle](/images/araf/araf_gn001_assessment_lifecycle.svg)

ARAF Assessment Lifecycle — Operational sequence applied by accredited assessors when conducting a governance architecture assessment under the Agentic Risk Architecture Framework (ARAF).

Step 7 in the lifecycle sequence: Governance Coherence Assessment (GCI) — required for Compliant and Certified determinations.

---

## Engagement Setup

Establish engagement terms, assessor independence, methodology version, and scope boundaries before evidence requests begin.

The engagement setup stage must confirm:

- assessor independence and conflict disclosure status
- assessment objective and intended institutional audiences
- methodology version applied and documentation baseline
- expected assessment outputs: dimensional profile, GBI score, multiplier analysis, and certification pathway position
- engagement governance protocol, including escalation contacts and document control

---

## Evidence Collection

Evidence collection must be chain-complete, contemporaneous where possible, and reconstructable by institutional reviewers.

Evidence tiers are defined as follows:

- Tier 1 — infrastructure-generated records
- Tier 2 — contemporaneous documentation
- Tier 3 — reconstructed documentation
- Tier 4 — management representation

Tier 4 evidence is management representation without contemporaneous supporting records.

Not admissible for coherence assessment at any certification tier.

---

## Dimensional Scoring

Assess governance posture across the six ARAF dimensions:

- D1 Autonomy Gradient
- D2 Data Sensitivity Exposure
- D3 Contract Infrastructure
- D4 Liability Architecture
- D5 Commercial Leverage
- D6 Adaptive Stability

Dimensional scoring output must include per-dimension reasoning, evidence tier basis, and any limitations affecting confidence in the score.

---

## Multiplier Assessment

Evaluate multiplier conditions after dimensional scoring and before final composite interpretation.

| Multiplier | Trigger Condition | Canonical Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Systemic Escalation | D1 &ge; 4 and D4 &ge; 4 | High autonomy with inadequate liability architecture. AE3 volume is high and liability framework is not designed to absorb consequences. |
| Infrastructure Collapse | D3 &ge; 4 and D1 &ge; 3 | Significant autonomy with inadequate contract infrastructure. Liability allocation at the execution boundary is contractually ungoverned. |
| Leverage Collapse | D5 &ge; 4 and D4 &ge; 3 | High commercial dependency with inadequate liability architecture. System becomes structurally resistant to remediation. |
| Political Cascade | D5 &ge; 4 and D3 &ge; 3 with single-provider dependency and government-adjacent customer concentration | High commercial dependency combined with inadequate political force majeure or provider substitution provisions governing government designation events. |

---

## GBI Calculation and Coherence Assessment

Calculate the Governance Benchmark Index (GBI) from the six-dimensional profile and apply multiplier interpretation where triggered.

For ARAF Compliant and ARAF Certified determination attempts, Governance Coherence Index (GCI) assessment is mandatory and must be evaluated over the required operational window.

The GBI and GCI role split is fixed:

- GBI assesses governance design adequacy
- GCI assesses operational coherence

---

## Report Drafting

Prepare a structured report with sufficient detail for boards, insurers, investors, regulators, and other institutional audiences to reconstruct assessment reasoning.

The report must include scope, methodology version, dimensional findings, multiplier findings, evidence basis, certification determination basis, and limitations.

Validity periods must be stated explicitly as follows:

- ARAF Assessed — reliance period up to 60 days from report issue date
- ARAF Compliant — certification valid up to 12 months
- ARAF Certified — certification valid up to 24 months subject to 12-month surveillance

---

## Certification Determination and Communication

Apply certification thresholds without modification and communicate determination with supporting dimensional and evidence rationale.

Thresholds:

- ARAF Certified — GBI ≤ 1.75 AND GCI ≥ 0.85
- ARAF Compliant — GBI ≤ 2.50 AND GCI ≥ 0.70
- ARAF Assessed — any GBI score

Certification communication must state the certification tier, score basis, evidence basis, validity period, and reassessment obligations.

---

## Post-Assessment Obligations

Post-assessment obligations apply to assessor records, surveillance, and reassessment trigger handling.

Minimum obligations include:

- communicate reassessment trigger obligations at determination issuance
- monitor and document triggering events for Compliant and Certified outcomes
- conduct 12-month surveillance for Certified outcomes
- retain assessment records to support quality assurance and dispute review

Reassessment trigger categories are maintained in the [Reassessment Trigger Taxonomy](/assessor-guidance/reassessment-trigger-taxonomy/).

---

## Appendix A (Report Template)

Use this template structure for all issued reports:

- Executive Summary
- Scope and Assessment Window
- Design Adequacy Assessment (GBI)
- Governance Coherence Assessment (GCI), where required
- Evidence Summary
- Findings and Observations
- Scope Limitation and Disclaimer
- Determination, Validity, and Reassessment Conditions

Validity periods (mandatory wording):

- ARAF Assessed — reliance period up to 60 days from report issue date
- ARAF Compliant — certification valid up to 12 months
- ARAF Certified — certification valid up to 24 months subject to 12-month surveillance
