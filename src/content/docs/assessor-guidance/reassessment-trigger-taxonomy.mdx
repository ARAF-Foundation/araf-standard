This taxonomy defines the standard reassessment trigger categories used in ARAF certification monitoring and post-assessment obligations.

It is used in conjunction with [GN-001: Conducting an ARAF Governance Assessment](/assessor-guidance/gn-001/), [GN-003: Governance Coherence Assessment](/assessor-guidance/gn-003/), and the [Certification Framework](/certification/certification-framework/).

## Trigger Categories

- material change to system autonomy level (increase of more than one Autonomy Gradient level)
- substitution of a primary AI provider or model
- significant expansion of operational scope beyond the assessed scope
- a material incident in which the governance architecture was engaged, tested, or found insufficient
- discovery of systematic governance drift outside the normal assessment cycle

## Trigger Handling Rule

Where a triggering event is notified, the accredited assessor determines whether a full reassessment is required or whether a targeted coherence re-evaluation is sufficient, and documents the basis for that determination.

Continued use of certification following a triggering event without initiating reassessment constitutes a certification breach.
