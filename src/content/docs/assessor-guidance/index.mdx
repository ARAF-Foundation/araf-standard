ARAF assessments are scoped to a defined deployment node. Any GBI score, certification status, or dimensional profile referenced on this page applies to the deployment node declared at assessment intake. Institutional reliance must be read against that declared scope.

This section provides guidance for two audiences: organisations preparing to undergo ARAF assessment, and accredited assessors conducting assessments under the ARAF methodology.

The resources below are organised accordingly.


## For organisations preparing for assessment

These resources address the threshold questions an organisation must work through before and during an ARAF engagement.

**Getting Started with ARAF Assessment** — What systems should be assessed, when assessment is appropriate, how long assessments typically take, who should lead the process internally, and how to prepare your evidence programme. A consolidated implementation guide for organisations at the beginning of the assessment pathway.

[Getting Started with ARAF Assessment →](/assessor-guidance/getting-started/)

**What to Expect During an Assessment** — A description of the assessment process from the organisation's perspective: how the assessor will engage, what evidence will be requested, how dimensional scoring works in practice, and how the certification determination is reached.

[What to Expect During an Assessment →](/assessor-guidance/what-to-expect/) *(Forthcoming)*

## For accredited assessors

These resources supplement the Assessor Accreditation Standard with operational methodology guidance. They do not replace the Accreditation Standard — that document governs who may assess and under what conditions. These guidance notes address how assessments are conducted.

**GN-001: Conducting an ARAF Governance Assessment** — End-to-end methodology for assessment practice, from engagement setup through report delivery. Covers system scoping, Decision Supply Chain mapping, evidence collection, dimensional scoring, multiplier assessment, Governance Benchmark Index calculation, coherence evaluation, and certification determination.

[GN-001: Conducting an ARAF Governance Assessment →](/assessor-guidance/gn-001/) *(Forthcoming)*

**GN-002: Cross-Border Deployment Assessment** — Supplementary methodology for deployments that span jurisdictions. Extends GN-001 Sections 2, 3, 5, and 7. Covers jurisdictional footprint mapping, dimensional scoring adjustments for cross-border deployments, regulatory exposure mapping, jurisdictional liability architecture, tax and commercial structuring implications, infrastructure dependency exposure, and brand and reputational architecture assessment. Apply in conjunction with GN-001, not as a standalone document.

[GN-002: Cross-Border Deployment Assessment →](/assessor-guidance/gn-002/) *(Forthcoming)*

**GN-003: Governance Coherence Assessment** — Supplementary methodology for operational coherence evaluation, including the three coherence components, evidence sampling procedures, the 180-day assessment window in practice, and GCI calculation. Required for all ARAF Compliant and ARAF Certified determinations where operational governance evidence must be evaluated over time.

[GN-003: Governance Coherence Assessment →](/assessor-guidance/gn-003/) *(Forthcoming)*
