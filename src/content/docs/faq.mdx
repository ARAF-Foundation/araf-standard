## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is ARAF?

The Agentic Risk Architecture Framework (ARAF) is an open governance standard for autonomous decision infrastructure. It provides a structured method for classifying governance posture, assessing evidence quality, and communicating institutional governance signals.

### Is ARAF a responsible AI framework?

ARAF is a governance architecture assessment framework. Responsible AI frameworks typically describe governance principles or ethical objectives such as transparency, fairness, and accountability. ARAF assesses whether the governance infrastructure required to achieve those objectives exists within a specific autonomous system deployment. Its purpose is to produce a measurable governance signal, the Governance Benchmark Index, that institutions can rely upon for oversight, underwriting, investment, and regulatory supervision.

Principles describe what governance should achieve. ARAF measures whether the architecture to achieve it is present.

### Is ARAF a technical safety framework?

No. ARAF is a governance architecture framework. It complements technical safety and security standards, but its focus is accountability architecture, evidence quality, and institutional reliance.

### Does ARAF assess model accuracy?

No. ARAF does not assess model accuracy. It evaluates governance architecture, accountability assignments, evidence quality, and reconstructability.

### Does ARAF guarantee safety?

No. ARAF does not guarantee safety outcomes. It strengthens governance and evidentiary accountability around autonomous decisions, but it does not replace system-level safety engineering, testing, or assurance.

### Does ARAF replace regulatory compliance?

No. ARAF does not replace regulatory compliance. It provides structured governance evidence that can support compliance work, but organisations remain responsible for meeting all applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

### How does ARAF relate to ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, and the EU AI Act?

ARAF complements these frameworks. ISO 42001 and NIST AI RMF provide process and risk-management structures, and the EU AI Act provides legal obligations. ARAF assesses whether the governance architecture of a specific deployment is institutionally accountable and reconstructable, then translates that assessment into a comparable governance signal (GBI and certification tiers).

### What does ARAF measure?

ARAF assesses governance posture across six dimensions:

- Autonomy Gradient
- Data Sensitivity Exposure
- Contract Infrastructure
- Liability Architecture
- Commercial Leverage
- Adaptive Stability

### What is the Governance Benchmark Index (GBI)?

GBI is the composite signal derived from dimensional assessment outputs. It uses a 1.0 to 5.0 scale, where lower scores indicate stronger governance posture.

### Does a single GBI number replace dimensional analysis?

No. The GBI provides comparability, while the dimensional profile provides diagnosis. Both are required to understand governance posture and remediation priorities.

### What are multiplier conditions?

Multiplier conditions identify structural risk interactions where dimensional weaknesses compound. These conditions can materially increase governance exposure beyond what isolated dimensional scores would suggest.

### What evidence quality tiers does ARAF use?

ARAF uses three evidence quality tiers:

- Infrastructure-generated evidence
- Contemporaneous documentation
- Reconstructed documentation

These tiers indicate confidence in governance claims and reconstructability of decision accountability.

### What is the difference between assessment and certification?

Assessment produces the governance outputs (dimensional profile, GBI, multiplier analysis, evidence quality). Certification is the market-facing signal issued by an accredited independent assessor based on that assessment.

### What are the certification tiers?

ARAF defines three tiers:

- ARAF Assessed
- ARAF Compliant (`GBI <= 2.50`)
- ARAF Certified (`GBI <= 1.75`)

### Who can issue ARAF certifications?

Only accredited assessors operating under the ARAF Assessor Accreditation Standard can issue certifications. Self-assessment does not satisfy the independence requirement for certification.

### How often should systems be reassessed?

Reassessment follows certification lifecycle requirements, including scheduled reassessment intervals and event-triggered reassessment when material governance changes occur.
