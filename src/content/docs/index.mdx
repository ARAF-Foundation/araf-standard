## Where governance actually happens

AI governance does not fail at the level of policy.
It fails at the moment a decision is made.

Inside organisations, consequential decisions are already being produced through a distributed chain of systems, data, contracts, and human approvals.
That chain rarely produces a complete, defensible record of how those decisions were made.

ARAF defines the structure required to make those decisions:

* traceable
* governable
* and defensible under scrutiny

---

## What breaks without governance architecture

Without structured governance:

* decisions cannot be reconstructed
* accountability is fragmented across systems and teams
* liability is unallocated or misaligned
* governance claims cannot be verified

This is not a policy gap.
It is an infrastructure gap.
