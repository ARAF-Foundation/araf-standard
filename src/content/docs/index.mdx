Scope of Governance

ARAF governs the decision infrastructure surrounding autonomous systems, not the internal technical performance of the models themselves.

Technical safety standards evaluate whether an AI system functions correctly.
ARAF evaluates whether the organisation deploying that system can demonstrate accountability for the decisions it produces.

This distinction runs throughout the framework and is operationalised through three core elements:

The Decision Supply Chain - the sequence of systems, human actors, and infrastructure through which a consequential decision is produced.

The Six Governance Dimensions - the structured methodology used to assess the governance posture of that decision infrastructure.

The Governance Benchmark Index (GBI) - the classification signal that translates governance posture into a comparable institutional metric.

Together, these components allow organisations to demonstrate that the decisions produced by autonomous systems can be reconstructed from contemporaneous governance records and evaluated in institutional terms.
