## Where governance actually happens

AI governance does not fail at the level of policy—it fails at the moment a decision is made.

Inside organisations, consequential decisions are produced through a distributed chain of systems, data, contracts, and human approvals. That chain rarely produces a complete, defensible record of how those decisions were made.

ARAF defines the structure required to make those decisions:

- traceable
- governable
- defensible under scrutiny

---

## What breaks without governance architecture

Without structured governance:

- Decisions cannot be reconstructed
- Accountability is fragmented across systems and teams
- Liability is unallocated or misaligned
- Governance claims cannot be verified

This is not a policy gap—it is an infrastructure gap.

---

## What ARAF produces

An ARAF assessment produces:

- A mapped Decision Supply Chain
- A six-dimensional governance profile
- A Governance Benchmark Index (GBI) score
- An evidence-backed governance record
- A certification pathway for institutional reliance
