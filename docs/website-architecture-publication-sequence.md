# Website Architecture and Publication Sequence

## Overview

The ARAF website is structured as a set of core pages that introduce the standard, explain how governance posture is evaluated, and define the institutional signals produced by the framework.

The site architecture currently includes six pages.

Two pages are already published:

- Governance Benchmark Index (GBI)
- Six Dimensions

The remaining four pages complete the framework explanation and should be published in a defined sequence to match how institutional audiences process the standard.

## Recommended Publication Sequence

### Recommended Next Page: Certification

The Certification page should be published next.

The GBI page explains how governance posture is scored.  
The Six Dimensions page explains what is assessed.

The immediate question both pages create for institutional readers is:

What does a score mean for my organisation?

Certification answers this question.

It converts the scoring model and dimensional framework into the three institutional signals produced by the standard:

- ARAF Assessed
- ARAF Compliant
- ARAF Certified

These tiers provide the signals that boards, insurers, investors, and procurement teams rely on when evaluating governance posture.

### External Documents That Depend on the Certification Page

Several external-facing materials already reference the certification tiers:

- Insurer Pitch Document
- Salesforce Design Partner Proposal
- Founder Assessment Brief

Each document assumes the reader can look up the certification tiers on the website.

Publishing the Certification page ensures these references resolve to a clear explanation of the institutional signal produced by the standard.

## Rationale for Page Order

The publication sequence should follow institutional reading logic, not internal drafting order.

For institutional audiences, the expected flow is:

- what is assessed
- how it is scored
- what the score produces
- who governs the standard

This sequence supports decision use by boards, insurers, enterprise buyers, and diligence teams.

### Standard Governance

The Standard Governance page should follow immediately after Certification.

This page answers the structural credibility question:

Who maintains the standard, and what prevents it from being adjusted to favour the implementation partner?

Standard Governance provides the institutional governance model that maintains the standard and ensures methodological integrity.

### Technical Architecture Pages

The remaining pages describe the deeper technical structure of the framework.

These pages are primarily relevant to:

- assessors
- technical reviewers
- organisations implementing the standard

They are not required for initial institutional understanding of the framework.

The two technical pages are:

- Decision Supply Chain
- Governance Coherence Addendum

Decision Supply Chain should be published first because it defines the governance object being assessed.

Governance Coherence Addendum explains how coherence is evaluated across the decision chain.

## Page Purpose Descriptions

- Governance Benchmark Index (GBI): Explains how governance posture is scored and how the composite signal should be interpreted.
- Six Dimensions: Defines what is assessed across the six governance dimensions and establishes dimensional structure for assessment.
- Certification: Converts dimensional and scoring outputs into institutional signals used in procurement, underwriting, oversight, and diligence.
- Standard Governance: Defines the governance model that maintains the standard and protects methodological integrity over time.
- Decision Supply Chain: Defines the distributed governance object under assessment and clarifies chain-level accountability structure.
- Governance Coherence Addendum: Defines the operational verification layer and how governance coherence is evaluated across the chain after design assessment.
